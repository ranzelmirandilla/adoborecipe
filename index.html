Philippine Chicken Adobo Recipe

Philippine Chicken Adobo is the staple dish of the Filipino culture. The adobo cooking process was first recorded when the Spaniards colonized the Philippines in the early 17th century. The cooking process can involve meat, fish, and vegetables and is commonly served with rice at meals or feasts.

Prep: 20 mins
Cook: 50 mins
Total: 1hr 10 mins

INGREDIENTS:

  • 4 to 5 pounds chicken thighs
  • 1/2 cup white vinegar
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 4 cloves of garlic, crushed
  • 1 teaspon black peppercorns
  • 3 bay leaves

DIRECTIONS:

  1. Combine the chicken thighs, vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, peppercorns and bay leaves in a large pot. Cover and marinate the chicken in the refrigerator for 1 to 3 hours.
  2. Bring the chicken to a boil over high heat. Lower the heat, cover and let simmer for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally. Remove the lid and simmer until the sauce is reduced and thickened and the chicken is tender, about 20 more minutes. Serve with steamed rice.
